• Switch to NOAC but keep the patient in hospital for a few days for further observation:
    • An oral tablet that works rapidly –as rapidly as a parenteral agent – to slow clot progression 1,2
    • Single-drug approach (rivaroxaban or apixaban) allows early discharge from hospital – no overlap with a parenteral agent is needed 3,5
    • Some NOACs, e.g. dabigatran, require 5–10 days’ parenteral anticoagulation before switching 6,7
    • 3 months’ initial treatment then re-assess every 3 months 3,8
    • Requirements: take tablet as directed at the same time(s) each day; review appointment with anticoagulation clinic/haematologist (at 21 days if using rivaroxaban to coincide with dose reduction from 15 mg bid to 20 mg od, or at 7 days if using apixaban to coincide with dose reduction from 10 mg bid to 5 mg bid); no known dietary restrictions or food interactions 9–12
    • ESC guidelines recommend patients with a PESI score of 0 may be suitable for management at home. For an sPESI score of 1 inpatient monitoring should be considered 3
