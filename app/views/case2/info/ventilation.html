• Assesses air and blood flow in the lungs using a radioactive gas and the injection of contrast dye, respectively 1
  • Q-scan results (blood flow) may be considered more appropriate here than V-scan results (air flow); if PE is suspected, use of CTPA scan is preferred 2
  • Utilizes radiolabelled albumin and inhaled gases to compare pulmonary perfusion and ventilation 1
  • Should only be considered when there is a clinical suspicion of PE 1
  • V/Q scanning has high sensitivity but much lower specificity than CTPA, particularly when other lung disease is present 3
  • May be used in patients with a history of allergy to contrast dye 1
