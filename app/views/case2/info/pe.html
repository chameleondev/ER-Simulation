• Clinical features of PE 3:
    • Shortness of breath: The patient may have difficulty catching their breath at rest, and the shortness of breath often worsens with activity
    • Plueritic chest pain: The pain is classically described as sharp, and it worsens when taking a deep breath
    • A cough that may produce bloody sputum (haemoptysis)
    • Abnormal vital signs: tachycardia, tachypnoea, hypoxia and cyanosis
