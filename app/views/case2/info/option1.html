• Switch to a NOAC and discharge the patient after 24 hours:
    • An oral tablet that works rapidly – as rapidly as a parenteral agent – to slow clot progression2,3
    • Single-drug approach (rivaroxaban or apixaban) allows early discharge from hospital – no overlap with a parenteral agent is needed. If LMWH has been administered initially, a switch to a single-drug NOAC (e.g. rivaroxaban) can follow at the same time 24 hours later (at hospital or home)4–7
    • Dabigatran and edoxaban require at least 5 days’ parenteral anticoagulation before switching8–11
    • 3 months’ initial treatment then re-assess every 3 months1,12,13
    • Requirements: take tablet as directed at the same time(s) each day; review appointment with anticoagulation clinic/haematologist (at 21 days if using rivaroxaban to coincide with dose reduction from 15 mg bid to 20 mg od, or at 7 days if using apixaban to coincide with dose reduction from 10 mg bid to 5 mg bid); no known dietary restrictions or food interactions4–7
    • ESC guidelines recommend patients with a PESI score of 0 may be suitable for management at home. With an sPESI score of 1, inpatient monitoring should be considered1
