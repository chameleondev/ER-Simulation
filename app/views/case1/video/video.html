    • 34-year-old maile presents with pain and swelling in the left leg
  • History of presenting complaint

  • Past medical history
    • Usually fit and healthy, normal BMI

  • Drug history

  • Family history
    • No family history of thromboembolism
  • Social history
    • Non-smoker/drug user, moderate alcohol intake
    • Computer analyst
    • No recent long haul flights

  • Review of systems
    • No other complaints

Assessment

  • Physical examination
    • Calf tenderness and general swelling
    • Normal heart rate
    • No chest pain or dyspnoea
    • No rash or signs of bruising
    • No other concerns
  • Routine blood tests
    • No renal impairment
    • Increased D-dimer
  • Two-level DVT Wells’ score
    • Paralysis, paresis or immobilization
    • Pain/tenderness along deep vein system
    • Swollen leg

Diagnostics

  • Thrombophilia test
    • Blood test to identify problems with the clotting cascade – a positive result may indicate the patient has thrombophilia, which increases the risk of VTE
    • Not required if provoked VTE likely
  • Chest X-ray
    • If the patient has symptoms including chest pain or breathing difficulties, a chest X-ray may help to diagnose heart or lung problems
    • Not required if patient is not symptomatic for PE
  • Ultrasound scan
    • Used to detect a clot in the veins of the leg
    • A Doppler ultrasound can detect how fast the blood is flowing through the leg and whether the flow is blocked or reduced
  • Anticoagulation therapy
    • If the test or scan cannot be performed straight away, LMWH or rivaroxaban should be administered whilst awaiting test results
  • Venogram
    • Can be used if D-dimer test and ultrasound scan are inconclusive
    • A contrast dye is injected into the foot and travels to the leg veins – a blood clot will appear as a dye-free ‘gap’ in the blood vessel
  • CTPA
    • Recommended test for diagnosing PE
    • CT visualization of blood vessels in the lungs using contrast dye
  • Ventilation/perfusion lung scan
    • Assesses air and blood flow in the lungs using a radioactive gas and the injection of contrast dye, respectively
    • Use CTPA first
