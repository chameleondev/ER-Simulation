Have you considered using rivaroxaban?

  • Rivaroxaban may overcome some of these limitations
    • no need for routine coagulation monitoring
    • no known dietary restrictions or food interactions
    • lower risk of bleeding
    • most patients can be treated at home (may allow earlier discharge from hospital)
    • once-daily regime after 3 weeks

Correct

  • Patient leaves hospital on rivaroxaban 15 mg twice daily, and arrange follow-up visit at a coagulation clinic for 21 days time, when the patient is switched to 20 mg for 3 months
  • No need for routine coagulation monitoring, no known dietary restrictions or food interactions, and most patients can be treated at home (may allow earlier discharge from hospital)
  • After 21 days, the patient moves to a once-daily regime (similar to VKAs) which improves compliance and is better suited for younger, active patients
  • Lower risk of major bleeding for rivaroxaban (46% reduction) compared with LMWH/VKA therapy
