Swollen legs

  • Check for bilateral swelling which indicates systemic disease (e.g. cardiac failure). Swelling may not be even in both legs
  • Unilateral swelling indicates a more localised cause (e.g. trauma, DVT)
  • Consider pregnancy, obesity or immobility as a cause of swollen legs

Local

  • Acute:
  • Trauma
  • DVT
  • Cellulitis
  • Allergic reaction
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Chronic:
  • Venous problem (e.g. varicose veins)
  • Lymphoedema
  • Congenital malformation
  • Stasis due to obesity or poor mobility

Systemic

  • Congestive cardiac failure
  • Hypoproteinaemia
  • Renal failure
  • Fluid overload
  • Anaemia
  • Drugs (e.g. NSAIDs)
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Hereditary angioneurotic
  • oedema
