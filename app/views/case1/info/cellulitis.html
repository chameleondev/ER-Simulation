• If infection of the dermis and/or subcutaneous tissue is suspected check for redness of the skin with poorly defined borders
  • Check for Streptococcus or Staphylococcus infection
  • Erysipelas is a type of cellulitis but the borders of infection are defined and the rash is more aggressive in appearance
  • Risk factors for cellulitis include:
    • Previous cellulitis
    • Venous insufficiency
    • Older age
    • Diabetes
    • Alcoholism
    • Immunodeficiency
    • Lymphoedema
    • Obesity
    • Insect bites
    • IV drug use
    • Skin abrasions
    • Inflammatory dermatoses
