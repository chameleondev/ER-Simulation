• Blood test that detects broken down components of a blood clot that remain in the blood
  • If D-dimer test is negative, this rules out the possibility of a DVT by up to 97%4
  • Clinical note: however, in 40–60% of patients, this may give a false-positive result in patients who:5
    • Have recently had surgery or a trauma
    • Have an inflammatory response
    • Are elderly
  • If you suspect DVT, try to identify an obvious cause or provoking factor
  • If the Wells’ score indicates that DVT is unlikely and the D-dimer is negative, do not do any further tests1,6,7
  • If the Wells’ score indicates that DVT is likely, confirm the diagnosis using an appropriate scanning technique without performing a D-dimer test, unless the scan is delayed8
